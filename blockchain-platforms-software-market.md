<p><p>Blockchain platforms can be categorized into three main types: public, private, and consortium. Public blockchains are open to anyone, allowing for transparent transactions and decentralization, exemplified by cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin. Private blockchains are restricted to select participants, prioritizing confidentiality and speed, making them suitable for businesses. Consortium blockchains are semi-private, governed by a group of organizations that collaborate while maintaining some control, offering a balance between transparency and privacy, often used in industries like finance and supply chains.</p></p>
