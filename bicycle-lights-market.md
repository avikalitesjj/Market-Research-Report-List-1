<p><p>The bicycle lights market is primarily divided into headlights and taillights, each serving distinct safety functions. Headlights illuminate the path ahead, enhancing visibility for cyclists at night or in low-light conditions, while also making them more visible to motorists. Taillights, positioned at the rear, signal a cyclist's presence to those behind, often equipped with flashing features to increase attention. Both types cater to various preferences in brightness, battery life, and mounting options, ensuring safety and compliance for cyclists in different environments.</p></p>
