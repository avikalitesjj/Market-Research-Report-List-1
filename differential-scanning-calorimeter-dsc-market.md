<p><p>Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC) is a thermal analysis technique used to measure the heat flow associated with phase transitions in materials as a function of temperature and time. The market includes two main types: Heat Flux DSC, which measures the temperature difference between a sample and a reference to determine heat flow, and Power-Compensation DSC, which maintains a constant temperature difference by adjusting the heating power, enabling precise calorimetric measurements. Both are essential for material characterization in various industries.</p></p>
