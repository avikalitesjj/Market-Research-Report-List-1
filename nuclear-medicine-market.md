<p><p>The nuclear medicine market is segmented based on isotope types used for diagnostic and therapeutic purposes. Tc-99 is the most widely used radionuclide for imaging due to its favorable half-life and availability. I-123 and I-131 are critical for thyroid-related diagnostics and treatments. In-111 is utilized in labeling monoclonal antibodies for cancer imaging, while Xe-133 is employed in pulmonary function tests. Th-201 is used in myocardial imaging, and Ga-67 aids in detecting infections and tumors. Additionally, other isotopes cater to specialized needs in nuclear medicine.</p></p>
