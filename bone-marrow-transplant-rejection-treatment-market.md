<p><p>Bone marrow transplant rejection treatment involves several pharmaceutical types, including Azathioprine, Adrenocorticotropic Hormone, Cyclophosphamide, Cyclosporine A, and others. Azathioprine is an immunosuppressant that reduces cell proliferation, while Adrenocorticotropic Hormone helps regulate adrenal hormone secretion to mitigate rejection. Cyclophosphamide is a potent immunosuppressant used to prevent graft rejection, and Cyclosporine A inhibits T-cell activation. Other treatments may include newer immunosuppressants or monoclonal antibodies, which target specific immune responses to enhance graft acceptance and improve patient outcomes.</p></p>
